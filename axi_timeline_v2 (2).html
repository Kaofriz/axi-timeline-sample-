I Ching (易経)
China
Systematic thinking on change, duality, and pattern
ⓄⓅ

Celtic / Druidic tradition
W. Europe, Ireland
Oral knowledge; sacred nature; fluid time (Samhain)

— Axial Age · c. 600–300 BCE
c. 500 BCE
Laozi (老子) AXI source ①
China
道可道，非恒道 — The speakable Tao is not the possible Tao (Yasutomi reading). Axes as open possibility.
Ⓦ
Spring & Autumn period — collapse of Zhou order; thought explosion mirrors crisis
Ⓟ
Hundred Schools of Thought (諸子百家) — parallel philosophical flowering
+
Resonance with AXI
First philosophical source of AXI. "Defined = dead" matches AXI's provisional axis model. Meta-perspective as opening, not closing.
Effective domain
Releasing attachment to fixed frames. Reducing over-determination. Recovery from single-axis fixation.
Difference from AXI
Laozi reaches meta and stays — non-action, silence. AXI cycles between meta and micro, operating provisionally at each level.
c. 500 BCE
Confucius (孔子)
China
Ren (仁) & Li (礼): responses calibrated to each interlocutor. Original teaching vs. later institutionalisation.
ⓇⓌ
Confucianism as state ideology (Han dynasty, 206 BCE) — coordinate transformation: dynamic teaching → fixed hierarchy
+
Resonance with AXI
Original Confucius: different responses for different interlocutors = local optimisation in practice. Li (礼) as shared coordinate system enabling communication efficiency.
Effective domain
Shared coordinate protocol within a group. Organisational efficiency. Formatted communication (academic writing, ceremony).
Difference from AXI
When Li is absolutised, it becomes an exclusionary power apparatus. AXI uses shared coordinates locally while retaining meta-perspective to relativise them.
c. 300 BCE
Zhuangzi (荘子)
China
Butterfly dream: observer coordinates are unfixable. "Uselessness of the useless" — multi-axis valuation.
Ⓟ
Daoist literary tradition — playful, paradoxical, anti-systematic; knowledge as lived experience
+
Resonance with AXI
Observer relativity (butterfly dream) = AXES observer-coordinate model. "Useless" from one axis = valuable from another — clearest pre-modern statement of multi-axis evaluation.
Effective domain
Releasing attachment to single-axis judgement. Creative and philosophical inquiry. Countering over-systematisation.
Difference from AXI
Zhuangzi dissolves boundaries but offers no return path. AXES provides provisional coordinates after boundary-dissolution — "now we know we're lost; here is a temporary map."
470–322 BCE
Socrates · Plato · Aristotle
Greece
Socrates: aporia as difference-recognition. Plato: fixed Idea. Aristotle: empirical classification, single-view limit.
ⒶⓅ
Athenian democracy & theatre — public space for contested ideas
+
Resonance with AXI
Socrates: "knowing you don't know" = recognising the gap between self and truth — difference-recognition. Aristotle: observation first, system second — matches AXI's empirical axis-generation.
Effective domain
Socratic method: education, guided inquiry. Aristotle: within-domain classification and deepening. Plato: ideal-type comparison for design and evaluation.
Difference from AXI
Plato's fixed Idea = absolute external axis; AXI's axes are generated and provisional. Aristotle cannot exit his own classification frame. Socrates steers toward a predetermined truth.
— Classical & Early Common Era · 300 BCE – 500 CE
c. 200 CE
Nāgārjuna (龍樹) AXI source ②
India
Śūnyatā: no axis has fixed substance. Two-truths: meta-relative / locally absolute. Zero lineage.
ⓈⓄ
Silk Road at peak — zero concept begins moving between India, China, Persia, and eventually Europe
Ⓡ
Mahāyāna Buddhism spreads across Central Asia toward China
+
Resonance with AXI
Two-truths doctrine is structurally identical to AXI: meta-level = all axes are relative (śūnya); local level = axes operate as provisional absolutes. Second philosophical source of AXI.
Effective domain
Philosophical grounding for non-attachment to fixed frames while still functioning in the world. Foundation for AXI's "provisional coordinate operation."
Difference from AXI
Two-truths in service of liberation (moksha). AXI uses the same structure for practical coordinate operation and sustained engagement — not liberation, but continuous combustion.
354–430 CE
Augustine of Hippo
North Africa / Rome
Time as structure of mind: memory / attention / expectation. Precursor to Kant's time-axis.
ⓌⓇ
Fall of Western Rome (476 CE) — coordinate collapse; Church fills vacuum
+
Resonance with AXI
Time located inside the mind — resonates with AXI's view that the time-axis is generated through experience (memory), not innate. Earliest Western version of this claim.
Effective domain
Christian faith context. Inner-life orientation. Stability through absolute axis for those in crisis.
Difference from AXI
Total dependence on God as absolute axis. AXI holds no absolute axis; even time is a provisional coordinate generated post-experientially.
— Medieval · 600–1400 CE
8–13c CE
Islamic Golden Age
Baghdad / Middle East
Al-Kindi, Ibn Sina, Ibn Rushd. Greek philosophy preserved & extended. Zero transmitted to Europe.
ⓈⒶ
House of Wisdom — multi-cultural translation hub; Greek + Indian + Persian synthesis
Ⓦ
Mongol destruction of Baghdad (1258) — intellectual closure; coordinate transformation of Islamic thought
+
Resonance with AXI
Monotheistic yet intellectually open — actively absorbed knowledge across cultural axes. Bridged Indian zero / Greek logic / Persian astronomy. Highest pre-modern example of multi-axis integration.
Effective domain
Cross-cultural knowledge synthesis. Translation and transmission. Scientific and philosophical bridge between ancient and modern.
Difference from AXI
Closure after Mongol destruction shows vulnerability of institutionalised knowledge. Same single-axis fixation risk: power monopolises what counts as knowledge.
13c CE
Thomas Aquinas
Italy
Reason deployed to prove a pre-set conclusion. Aristotle via Ibn Rushd.
ⓇⓅ
University system emerges — knowledge institutionalised under Church authority; scholasticism as epistemic protocol
+
Resonance with AXI
Systematic attempt to integrate Aristotle's empiricism with theology — cross-tradition synthesis has value. The logical apparatus is impressive even if the conclusion is pre-determined.
Effective domain
Within a shared faith coordinate system. Internal coherence of a belief structure. Useful where God-axis is already shared.
Difference from AXI
Conclusion precedes evidence — absolute axis is the premise, not the finding. AXI generates axes from difference-contact; no conclusion is fixed in advance.
— Early Modern · 1300–1700
14–16c
Renaissance
Italy / Europe
Shift from God-centred to human-centred axis. New absolute replaces old absolute.
ⓌⓇ
Black Death (1347–51) — 1/3 of Europe lost; Church authority shaken; same crisis-then-flowering pattern as Axial Age
ⓈⒶ
Gutenberg press (c.1440) & Medici patronage — knowledge diffusion; art, anatomy, perspective as empirical inquiry
Ⓢ
Copernicus (1543) — fixed Earth-axis dismantled; "Copernican turn" later echoed by Kant
+
Resonance with AXI
Collapse of existing absolute axis (Church) followed by explosion of new axes — identical pattern to Axial Age and Hundred Schools. Crisis generates epistemic diversity.
Effective domain
Transition periods. Cross-disciplinary synthesis. Conditions where patronage enables experimental inquiry without fixed institutional constraint.
Difference from AXI
Human-centrism replaces God-centrism — same absolute axis structure, different content. AXI does not replace one absolute with another; it pluralises axes provisionally.
17c
Descartes · Hume
France / Scotland
Descartes: subject as fixed origin. Hume: causality as habit — radical empiricism.
Ⓢ
Newton's Principia (1687) — classical mechanics as local absolute; later relativised by Einstein
+
Resonance with AXI
Hume's radical doubt of causality — "self-evident structures may be habits" — aligns with AXI's refusal to treat any axis as innate. Starting from doubt is a form of difference-recognition.
Effective domain
Descartes: establishing stable ground when all else is uncertain. Hume: questioning over-confident causal claims.
Difference from AXI
Descartes fixes "subject" as absolute starting point. AXI treats the subject as one axis among many. Hume's scepticism, taken too far, dissolves the ground for any provisional operation — AXI retains it.
— Modern · 1700–1900
18c
Kant
Prussia
Cognitive frameworks precede experience (a priori). "Container before content." AXI: containers are generated by difference-contact, not innate.
ⓌⓅ
French Revolution (1789) — Enlightenment ideals in political reality; "human rights" as new shared axis
+
Resonance with AXI
"Container (framework) first" insight is correct and important — how we perceive depends on what axes we already have. Copernican turn: cognition shapes what is seen, not the reverse.
Effective domain
Explaining why different observers reach different conclusions from the same phenomenon. Grounding for epistemic humility.
Difference from AXI
Kant treats time / space / causality as innate (a priori). AXI: all axes are generated through experience — including time (which requires memory). No fixed container comes pre-installed.
19c
Hegel · Marx · Nietzsche
Germany
Hegel: contradiction as fuel. Marx: material axis privileged. Nietzsche: God dead → sought new absolute → structural collapse.
ⓈⓌ
Industrial Revolution — technology reshapes social axes; labour / capital divide emerges
Ⓢ
Darwin's Origin of Species (1859) — biological axis of diversity as resilience; reinforces AXI's difference-as-condition-of-knowledge
+
Resonance with AXI
Hegel: contradiction (difference) as the engine of progress — AXI agrees. Marx: material conditions shape thought — one valid axis. Nietzsche: value must be self-generated, not inherited — resonates with AXI's axis-generation model.
Effective domain
Hegel: historical and dialectical analysis. Marx: economic and structural critique. Nietzsche: confronting value-vacuum after absolute loss.
Difference from AXI
All three privilege a single axis (Spirit / economy / will). AXI treats each as locally valid, not universally dominant. Nietzsche's breakdown illustrates the risk of seeking a new absolute rather than pluralising axes.
— 20th Century
1905–1915
Einstein AXI source ③
Germany / Switzerland
No absolute frame. Values are relative; local optimisation valid within defined domain. Scientific foundation for AXI's coordinate model.
Ⓢ
Quantum mechanics (1920s) — observer affects observation; reinforces situated knowledge; Newtonian mechanics remains valid locally
+
Resonance with AXI
Relativity = different observers, different coordinate systems, different values — all equally valid. Classical mechanics as local optimisation within its domain. Third and scientific source of AXI.
Effective domain
Physical science. Philosophical grounding for value-relativity. The Newton / Einstein example is AXI's clearest illustration of local optimisation.
Difference from AXI
Relativity describes physical reality. AXI extends the coordinate-system insight to epistemology, ethics, culture, and AI alignment — applying the structure beyond physics.
20c mid–late
Wittgenstein · Lévi-Strauss · Foucault · Derrida
Europe
Language / structure / power / différance. Meaning unfixable. AXI: agrees — but proposes provisional coordinate operation rather than silence or deconstruction alone.
Ⓢ
Computing & information theory (Turing, Shannon, 1940s–50s) — new axis of machine cognition emerges
Ⓦ
Cold War — liberal-individualist vs. collectivist epistemic premises in global confrontation
+
Resonance with AXI
Derrida: meaning drifts — matches AXI's provisional axis model. Foucault: knowledge-power entanglement — axis-setting is always a political act. Lévi-Strauss: deep structural patterns across cultures — AXI calls this shared axis-generation mechanism.
Effective domain
Deconstructing fixed meanings. Exposing power embedded in knowledge claims. Identifying cross-cultural structural commonalities.
Difference from AXI
Deconstruction without construction leaves no operational ground. AXI: after exposing that axes are provisional, we still need to choose and use them. Silence (Wittgenstein) and endless deferral (Derrida) are not sufficient for practice.
— Contemporary · 2000s–
2000s–
Meillassoux · Harman · Morton · Gabriel
France / USA / Germany
Post-human turn; objects relate without human observers; dark ecology; no meta-frame contains all. Converges with AXI from a Western path.
Ⓢ
Large language models (2017–) — high-dimensional feature spaces already encode coordinate structures; AXES proposes making them explicit and shareable
Ⓟ
AXI / AXES proposed (2026) — coordinate protocol for human↔human, human↔AI, AI↔AI communication across all three layers
+
Resonance with AXI
Harman OOO: non-human actors have their own coordinate relations — AXES extends to AI and objects. Morton: humans are one node in a mesh, not the centre. Gabriel: no meta-frame contains all — AXI agrees, and this is why AXES maps locally rather than universally.
Effective domain
Post-anthropocentric ethics. Ecological thinking. Multi-agent AI systems. Situations requiring non-human perspectives.
Difference from AXI
These Western thinkers arrive at similar positions through long philosophical detour. AXI synthesises them with Laozi, Nāgārjuna, and Einstein, and adds a practical coordinate protocol — AXES — for operationalisation.
